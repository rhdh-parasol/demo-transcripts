2. The Critical Conflict: Messaging

This is the aha moment — the two domains have directly contradictory messaging mandates:

Domain Messaging Rule Rationale
Claims AMQ Streams (Kafka) preferred for all async domain events Claim lifecycle events (FNOL created, status changed, payment approved) must flow through Kafka
Billing & Payments IBM MQ required for all payment transaction messaging Guaranteed exactly-once delivery for financial transactions. Kafka's at-least-once semantics are explicitly called out as insufficient

Our reconciliation service sits at the intersection of both domains: - It consumes claim settlement events (originating from Claims → Kafka) - It processes payment transactions (governed by Billing → IBM MQ)

Resolution: Dual-broker architecture. The service must bridge both messaging systems: 1. Consume claim lifecycle events from AMQ Streams (Kafka) — this is where Claims publishes "payment approved" events 2. Produce/consume payment transaction messages on IBM MQ — this is the Billing-mandated path for actual money movement 3. The reconciliation logic compares records from both streams to detect mismatches
