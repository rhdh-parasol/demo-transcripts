★ Insight ───────────────────────────────────── Phase 4 — Template Selection:

Three templates are available: parasol-python-service (FastAPI), parasol-nodejs-service (Express), and parasol-quarkus-service (Quarkus/Java).

The choice is unambiguous: parasol-quarkus-service. Both domains mandate Java/Quarkus for transactional services. Python is restricted to batch/ML workloads, Node.js to I/O-bound non-transactional work. A payment reconciliation service is squarely transactional.

The template scaffolds a Maven project with REST endpoint, health checks, metrics, and a catalog-info.yaml — and accepts parameters for owner, system, groupId, artifactId, javaPackageName, and port. ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

★ Insight ───────────────────────────────────── Key architectural takeaway: The most consequential discovery from this catalog exploration is the messaging conflict between domains. In a real enterprise, this is exactly the kind of cross-domain governance tension that the Backstage catalog is designed to surface before you start writing code.
